Hammamet

Hammamet is often presented as a seaside resort, one of the main in the country. The recent development of the Yasmine Hammamet marina reinforces the importance of the tourism industry in the local economy. Indeed, known since the second half of the twentieth century, the city offers tourists long beaches along the gulf bearing its name.

Archaeological Site

Neapolis is considered one of the few cities in the Maghreb to have a Greek name; it is also mentioned as early as the fifth century BC in the texts of Thucydides. It is also the city most anciently mentioned after Carthage and the same texts confirm its importance during the Punic period, before its destruction by Lucius Calpurnius Piso in 148 BC. The city was elevated to the rank of colony under Caesar or Augustus. Until the 5th century, some inscriptions still testify to its economic prosperity. The last data on the city are related to the attestation of bishops until 6461. This economic prosperity was mainly based on the production of a condiment very popular among the Romans and made from fish, the garum. The city was particularly affected by a major earthquake that caused a tidal wave in July 365, which submerged an entire part of the city whose remains (about twenty hectares) were discovered in 2017 by an Italian-Tunisian archaeological mission. It was not until the 12th century that Al Idrissi mentions the existence of an «ancient Nabeul» reduced to ruins a few kilometers from the new city.

Klibia

Klibia is also an important fishing port with an annual production of 15,000 tonnes of various seafood, including about 15% of Tunisian fish production; Kélibia is particularly specialized in lamparo fishing.

Archaeological Site

The fort of Kélibia is a 16th century citadel located on a rocky promontory 150 meters high that dominates the Mediterranean Sea and the city of Kélibia in the governorate of Nabeul, on the north-eastern coast of the Tunisian peninsula of Cap Bon. The building, which is surrounded by a wall reinforced with square towers, can be accessed through a gate defended by a barbican. The building also houses a lighthouse in its south corner. The fort is now one of the main tourist attractions in the city of Khelibia.
